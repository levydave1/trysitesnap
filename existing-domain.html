GoDaddy Walkthrough

  1. Log in to your GoDaddy Portfolio.
  2. Find your domain and click the DNS button.
  3. Update the A Record (@) to 76.76.21.21.
  4. Point the CNAME (www) to cname.vercel-dns.com.

The Detailed Path:

After logging in, look for the "Domain" sidebar. In your list of domains, there is a small "DNS" link next to each name. Once inside, you'll see a table of records. Look specifically for Type "A" where the Name is "@". Click the pencil icon to modify it.

Expert Tips:

  • • Delete any existing "Parked" records that might conflict.
  • • If you have an existing "A" record for "@", edit it instead of adding a new one.
  • • Standard TTL should be 1 hour.

Namecheap Walkthrough

  1. Log in and select Domain List.
  2. Click Manage next to your domain.
  3. Select the Advanced DNS tab.
  4. Create an A Record (@) ⮕ 76.76.21.21.

The Detailed Path:

Once in the Advanced DNS tab, you will see a red button labeled "Add New Record". Click it, select "A Record" from the dropdown, and enter "@" as the Host. Paste the IP address and make sure to click the small green checkmark to save.

Expert Tips:

  • • Ensure "TTL" is set to Automatic.
  • • Remove any "URL Redirect" records as they will block SiteSnap from loading.

Google / Squarespace Walkthrough

  1. Open your DNS Settings.
  2. Find Custom Records and click Add.
  3. A Record (@) ⮕ 76.76.21.21.
  4. CNAME (www) ⮕ cname.vercel-dns.com.

The Detailed Path:

If you were migrated from Google Domains to Squarespace, look for the "Domain Settings" icon. Click "DNS" and then "DNS Settings". You'll likely see a list of "Google Workspace" records—leave those alone and only add your custom records at the bottom.

Expert Tips:

  • • Squarespace Defaults: You must toggle off "Default Records" before adding SiteSnap records.

Universal Walkthrough

  1. Find your provider's DNS Zone Editor.
  2. Point the A Record (@) to 76.76.21.21.
  3. Point the CNAME (www) to cname.vercel-dns.com.

Global CDN Logic:

By connecting to these records, you are utilizing our global Edge Network. This ensures your site loads from the server closest to your visitor, reducing latency to near-zero.

Propagation (the time it takes for the world to see the change) varies by provider but usually completes within minutes.
